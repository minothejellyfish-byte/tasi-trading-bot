3. **Volume confirmation at entry time beats prior-day vol ratio** — wait for first candle to confirm volume is live
4. **Flat days are fine — the system's job is to not blow up** — −1.1% on a −0.22% index day is acceptable for a first sim; no stops needed, no panic

## Simulation 3 — Live Feed vs 15-Min Delayed Feed
*Tested 2026-05-15 — same Thursday data, same exit rules*

### Mechanics
- **Live**: signal fires at time T using data up to T; order executes at T price
- **Delayed**: signal fires at time T using data up to T−15min; order still executes at current live price
- The delay costs you on entry timing (you enter after the move starts) and exit timing (you exit after it already worsened)

### Results

| Feed | Trades | Total P&L | % of capital |
|------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Live (real-time) | 2 | −0.84 SAR | −0.21% |
| 15-min delay (yfinance) | 2 | −1.38 SAR | −0.34% |
| **Delay cost** | | **+0.54 SAR** | **+0.13%** |

### Entry/exit timing impact

| Stock | Live entry | Delayed entry | Slip | Live exit | Delayed exit |
|-------|-----------|---------------|------|-----------|--------------|
| 3060.SR | 10:05 @ 15.87 | 10:20 @ 15.84 | −0.03/share | 10:10 | 10:45 |
| 2130.SR | 10:25 @ 12.80 | 10:40 @ 12.87 | +0.07/share worse | 10:55 | 11:10 |

2130.SR was the key case: entered 15 min late at 12.87 instead of 12.80 → −0.90 SAR extra loss on that trade alone.

### Break-even analysis for real-time subscription (69 SAR/month)
- ~22 trading days/month → need to save **3.14 SAR/day** to break even
- On a down day: delay cost ~0.54 SAR → not break-even yet
- On a winning day, delay also costs exit timing (give back gains held 15 min too long)
- Break-even capital threshold: **~1,000–2,000 SAR** deployed

### Decision
Run first 2–3 live sessions on **delayed feed (yfinance)**. Review actual P&L vs simulated.
If capital raised to 1,000+ SAR, subscribe Derayah real-time (69 SAR/month) — delay cost
will exceed subscription fee on a single good session.
